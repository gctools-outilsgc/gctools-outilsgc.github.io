About Code GCconnex

What is Code GCconnex?

  • Code GCconnex is an event in which GC employees will collaborate to improve GCconnex by crowd sourcing resources and skills from computer programmers, designers, project managers, writers/editors, artists, team builders - or anyone who has an innovative idea they would like to see integrated into the platform.
  • The goal of the Code GCconnex is to allow members of the GC to build a better GCconnex. Employees from every area of the GC will be invited not only to submit their vision of an improved GCconnex experience but will also be encouraged to provide the necessary coding and design for enhancements.
  • GCconnex is scheduled for a significant update in 2016, which will include; improved performance, a “new look”, enhanced functionality and improved usability. To further improve the platform, the Service and GC2.0 Policy and Community Enablement (SGCPCE) Division at TBS is organizing a codefest event on December 9 and 10, 2015.
  • In advance of the codefest, and, in order to build capacity, the GC2.0 Tools team will host virtual workshops on coding and design.

Brief History of GCconnex

  • GCconnex, located within the secure Government of Canada firewall, was introduced to public servants in January 2009 as a professional networking platform for connecting and collaborating. The GCconnex platform played a significant role in engaging public servants and facilitating their involvement in enterprise-wide Blueprint 2020 activities.
  • There are more than 70,000 registered users on GCconnex. The public service at large is embracing the use of digital collaboration platforms, reflecting the emergence of a more agile, connected, innovative and responsive workforce. The GC2.0 Tools enable inter-departmental collaboration and team building through internal social networking tools.
